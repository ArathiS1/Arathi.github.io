Project Overview

This project presents an end-to-end predictive analytics solution designed to tackle customer churn in the telecommunications industry. By leveraging machine learning and advanced analytics, this system identifies at-risk customers, uncovers the root causes of churn, and provides actionable insights for retention strategies.

The analysis goes beyond traditional reporting by delivering a operational framework that enables targeted customer retention efforts, ultimately reducing marketing spend waste and increasing customer lifetime value.

Problem Statement

A telecom company is experiencing high customer churn but lacks a clear understanding of why customers are leaving and which customers are most likely to churn next.

This leads to inefficient retention efforts, wasted marketing spend, and continued revenue loss. Without a data-driven approach, the company was unable to:

  • Identify at-risk customers before they churned
  • Understand the key factors driving churn behavior
  • Allocate retention resources efficiently
  • Develop targeted retention strategies
  • Measure the effectiveness of retention campaigns

Key Insights from EDA & Modeling

The analysis revealed several critical factors influencing customer churn behavior

Contract Type is King

Month-to-month customers are over 300% more likely to churn than those on 2-year contracts. This was the strongest predictor of churn behavior, indicating the importance of long-term commitments for customer retention.

The Fiber Optic Paradox

Customers with Fiber optic internet have a churn rate ~40% higher than DSL customers, suggesting potential service issues with premium offerings despite the higher speed and performance expectations.

Tenure is Protective

Customers with less than 6 months tenure are ~4x more likely to churn than veterans with over 2 years, highlighting the importance of early retention efforts and onboarding experiences.

Methodology & Process

A comprehensive six-step approach was followed to ensure robust analysis and actionable results

1

Data Acquisition & Cleaning

Sourced customer data from a public Telco dataset. Engineered new features like avg_monthly_calc and handled missing values in TotalCharges. Performed comprehensive data quality checks and validation.

2

Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

Uncovered initial insights into key churn drivers like contract type and internet service through comprehensive visualization and statistical analysis. Identified patterns and correlations in the data.

3

Feature Engineering & Preprocessing

Built a robust pipeline to handle numeric and categorical features, including imputation, scaling, and one-hot encoding. Created new features that enhanced model performance and business interpretability.

4

Predictive Modeling

Trained and evaluated multiple models including Logistic Regression, Random Forest, and XGBoost. A RandomForest classifier was optimized via GridSearch, achieving superior performance with an AUC of 0.85.

5

Model Interpretation

Used Permutation Importance and SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations) to explain the model's reasoning for each customer. This provided both global and local interpretability for business stakeholders.

6

Deployment & Actionability

Scored customers and segmented them into risk deciles, enabling prioritized intervention and resource allocation. Developed a framework for ongoing monitoring and model retraining.

Results & Business Impact

The analysis provided clear, actionable insights that could directly inform customer retention strategy

High-Performance Predictor

The optimized RandomForest model achieved an ROC AUC of 0.85 and Average Precision of 0.65 on the test set, significantly outperforming baseline models and providing reliable churn predictions.

Key Drivers Identified

SHAP analysis revealed that contract type was the strongest predictor of churn. Higher monthly charges and the presence of Fiber optic service were also major contributors to customer attrition.

Proactive Retention Enabled

The model successfully concentrated risk: Over 50% of all actual churn events came from the top 2 deciles (20% of customers), enabling a 2.5x increase in retention campaign efficiency.

Strategic Recommendations

Based on the analysis, these strategic initiatives can significantly reduce churn rates

Targeted Interventions

Prioritize outreach to high-risk customers identified by the model (e.g., those in the top decile with month-to-month contracts). Develop personalized retention offers based on their usage patterns and reason for churn risk.

Product Bundling

Address the churn risk associated with Fiber optic by creating promoted bundles that include Tech Support or Online Security. Consider service quality improvements for fiber customers to enhance satisfaction.

Loyalty Programs

Incentivize customers on month-to-month plans to switch to longer-term contracts through discounted pricing, exclusive features, or loyalty rewards programs. Focus on building long-term customer relationships.
