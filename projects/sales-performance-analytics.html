Overview

Problem Statement

  • Delayed decision-making due to manual report consolidation.
  • Ineffective resource allocation across products and regions.
  • Inability to proactively address churn and return-rate issues.

Solution & Objectives

  • Identify Key Growth Levers: Top products, regions, and customer segments.
  • Diagnose Operational Issues: Return rates and customer behavior.
  • Track Performance Trends: YoY growth and AOV.
  • Enable Data-Driven Decisions: Self-service analytics for sales & marketing.

Methodology & Process

  1. Data Acquisition & Cleaning: Extracted from SQL DB.
  2. Data Modeling: Star schema with Fact_Sales + dimensions.
  3. DAX & Measure Engineering: Total Sales LY, YoY % Growth, Return Rate, AOV.
  4. Interactive Visualization: Drillable dashboard to transactional detail.
  5. Insight Synthesis: Actionable business recommendations.

SQL ‚Äì Data Wrangling & Analysis

-- Example Query 1: YoY Growth by Product
WITH SalesByYear AS (
    SELECT ProductName, YEAR(OrderDate) AS OrderYear, SUM(SalesAmount) AS TotalSales
    FROM Fact_Sales fs
    JOIN Dim_Product dp ON fs.ProductKey = dp.ProductKey
    GROUP BY ProductName, YEAR(OrderDate)
)
SELECT ProductName, OrderYear, TotalSales,
       LAG(TotalSales) OVER (PARTITION BY ProductName ORDER BY OrderYear) AS SalesLY,
       (TotalSales - LAG(TotalSales) OVER (PARTITION BY ProductName ORDER BY OrderYear)) /
       LAG(TotalSales) OVER (PARTITION BY ProductName ORDER BY OrderYear) * 100 AS YoYGrowth
FROM SalesByYear
ORDER BY ProductName, OrderYear;

-- Example Query 2: Top 10 Customers by Sales
SELECT TOP 10 CustomerID, SUM(SalesAmount) AS TotalSales,
       RANK() OVER (ORDER BY SUM(SalesAmount) DESC) AS CustomerRank
FROM Fact_Sales
GROUP BY CustomerID
ORDER BY TotalSales DESC;
